Classic French Madeleines

Small French sponge cakes baked in shell-shaped molds with a soft, buttery interior and lightly crisp exterior. Enjoy fresh from the oven or store at room temperature to experience their sweet, crispy flavor. Though simple in ingredients, their airy texture and elegant shape make them feel refined in presentation.

Recipe Overview

General Info

  • Prep Time: 1 hour, 15 minutes
  • Bake Time: 10–12 minutes
  • Total Time: 1 hour, 30 minutes
  • Yield: 18-20 madeleines
  • Serving Size: 2 madeleines
  • Difficulty: Beginner–Intermediate

Required Equipment

  • Madeleine pan (shell-shaped mold)
  • Hand or stand mixer
  • Mixing bowls
  • Whisk
  • Spatula
  • Zester (if using lemon zest)
  • Cooling rack

Ingredients

  • ½ cup (8 Tbsp; 113g) unsalted butter, plus 2 Tbsp (28g) for greasing the pan
  • 2 large eggs, at room temperature
  • ½ cup (100g) granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons lemon zest (adds brightness and traditional flavor)
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1 cup (115g) sifted all-purpose flour (spooned and leveled)
  • ½ teaspoon baking powder
  • ⅛ teaspoon salt
  • Optional: confectioners’ sugar, for dusting

Dietary Restriction

Contains: Dairy, Eggs, Gluten
Not gluten-free. Not dairy-free.

Instructions

  1. Melt ½ cup (113g) butter and set aside to cool slightly.
  2. Using a mixer, beat eggs and sugar on high speed for about 8 minutes until pale, thick, and ribbon-like.
  3. Mix in lemon zest and vanilla.
  4. Whisk flour, baking powder, and salt together in a separate bowl.
  5. Gently fold dry ingredients into egg mixture in two additions.
  6. Stir ¼ cup of the batter into the melted butter, then fold the butter mixture back into the remaining batter until smooth.
  7. Cover and chill the batter for 30–60 minutes.
  8. Preheat oven to 350°F (177°C). Brush/grease the madeleine pan with the remaining melted butter.
  9. Spoon 1 generous tablespoon of batter into the center of each mold without spreading.
  10. Bake for 10–12 minutes, until the tops spring back when lightly pressed.
  11. Invert the pan to release the madeleines and transfer to a rack to cool for 5-10 minutes before serving.
  12. Optional: Dust with confectioners’ sugar, if desired, and serve warm.

Serving, Storage, Timing Guide

Before You Begin

  • Bring eggs to room temperature (essential for proper volume).
  • Melt butter first and allow it to cool slightly.
  • Whip eggs and sugar for a full 8 minutes until thick and ribbon-like.
  • Chill batter for 30–60 minutes (do not chill longer).
  • Grease madeleine pan with melted butter, even if nonstick.
  • Preheat oven to 350°F (177°C) during the final minutes of chilling.

Best served: Warm, cool for about 5–10 minutes after baking.

Storage: Store in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days. Madeleines are best enjoyed the day they are baked, as they tend to dry out quickly. Freezing is not recommended, as it significantly affects texture.

Begin preparing batter around 1 hour 30 mins before serving. This allows time for mixing, chilling, baking, and slight cooling before serving warm.
